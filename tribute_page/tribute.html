Dr. Tetteh Quarshie

The man who brought cocoa to Ghana

Early Life In Ghana

  • Born in 1842.
  • He is from the Ga-Dangme ethnic group in Ghana
  • Father was a farmer called Teshie and mother was called Ashong-Fio
  • Started as an apprentice at a workshop at Akropong
  • First master backsmith at Akuapim-Mampong
  • He loved to farm with his father

Life Outside Ghana

  • Tetteh Quarshie left Ghana in 1870 to the Spanish Colony Fernando Po which now called Bioko in Equatorial Guinea
  • He came back to Ghana six year later with cocoa beans

Cocoa in Ghana

  • Tetteh Quarshie planted the first seeds at a farm in Mampong
  • He then distributed the cocoa seeds mto his friends and relatives. Soon, other farmers followed in their suit and then cocoa farming spread in Ghana. At this point, a missionary called Babel Missionaries started to import large quantities of the cocoa into the country
  • Exportation of cocoa from Ghana began in 1891 and a large scale exportation began in 1893
  • Later, cocoa was being exported to other countries like Nigeria , Sierra Leone and other African countries.
  • As time went on, Ghana provided almost half of the cocoa in the world
  • Between 1910 and 1980 Ghana was the world's largest cocoa provider

Death

  • Tetteh Quarshie died in 1892 at the age of 50
  • After his death, he was celebrated and known by many Ghananians as the man who brought cocoa to Ghana
  • His family and relatives were taken care of by the government through a grant
  • A schorlarship was set up at Achimota College in his honor
  • After the independence of Ghana (1957), a hospital wasm built in memorial of his death. The hospital was built in the Eatern Region of Ghana in Mampong-Akwapim called TETTEH QUARSHIE MEMORIAL HOSPITAL
